## Why "Ving Tsun" Not "Wing Chun"

The spelling "Ving Tsun" was officially adopted in **1967** when Ip Man and senior students founded the **Ving Tsun Athletic Association** in Hong Kong, partly to avoid the association with "WC" (a British term for toilet). Wong Shun Leung's students specifically use this spelling to honor the lineage. All variations — Wing Chun, Ving Tsun, Wing Tsun, WingTsun — refer to the same martial art; spellings indicate lineage affiliation. The site should use both "Ving Tsun" and "Wing Chun" for SEO purposes.

## Lineage & History

**Full lineage chain:**
Leung Yee Tai → Dr. Leung Jan (King of Wing Chun, Foshan) → Leung Bik → **Ip Man** → **Wong Shun Leung** → **Sifu Chan Kim Man** → **Sifu Wong Kin Wai**

**Ip Man** brought Ving Tsun from Foshan, China to Hong Kong. His student **Wong Shun Leung (1935–1997)** became one of the most celebrated fighters of his era, earning the nickname *"Gong Sau Wong"* (King of Talking Hands) from the Hong Kong press after an undefeated record across 100+ bare-knuckle challenge fights (beimo) against practitioners of Choy Li Fut, Hung Gar, Northern Shaolin, Karate, Judo, Boxing, and more. He is also credited with training Bruce Lee. His approach — calling Ving Tsun "scientific street fighting" — was built on proof in real combat, not tradition alone.

**Sifu Chan Kim Man** trained under Wong Shun Leung from 1978 until Wong's death in 1997 (19 years). He competed successfully from 1979–1982, has been teaching since 1995 in Guangzhou and Hong Kong, and runs **Shun Mo Scientific Ving Tsun** (淳武詠春拳學). His school is officially named *"Chan Kim Man Scientific Ving Tsun"* and carries on Wong Shun Leung's scientific, application-first philosophy.

**Sifu Wong Kin Wai (黃健偉)** trained under Sifu Chan Kim Man and is certified by the **Ving Tsun Athletic Association** (certificate #HK-02200, valid 8-Jun-2025 to 7-Jun-2028) as a recognized Ving Tsun Kungfu instructor. He is **the first and only Shun Mo Scientific Ving Tsun representative in the United States**, endorsed personally by Sifu Chan Kim Man. He has been teaching in Boston for several years.

## What Makes This Lineage Unique

- **Combat-proven:** Developed through 100+ real challenge fights, not theoretical tradition
- **Scientific approach:** Emphasis on understanding *why* techniques work, not rote memorization
- **50/50 weight distribution** (vs. traditional 70/30) — influenced by Wong Shun Leung's boxing background, prioritizing agility and mobility
- **Smaller, mastered curriculum** — fewer techniques executed with precision and speed rather than a broad repertoire
- **Modern training tools:** Focus mitts, kick shields, contact drills — bridges traditional kung fu with MMA-style conditioning
- **Direct lineage connection:** Sifu Chan Kim Man trained 19 years under Wong Shun Leung himself; Sifu Wong Kin Wai is his direct student

## Core Principles

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| Centerline Theory | Control the direct line to the opponent's vital targets (throat, solar plexus, groin) |
| Economy of Motion | Minimum force for maximum effect — no wasted movement |
| Simultaneous Attack & Defense | Attack while defending, never one or the other |
| Chi Sao (Sticky Hands) | Sensitivity-based training to read and react to an opponent's energy in real time |
| Relaxation & Structure | Tension kills sensitivity; stay rooted but relaxed |
| No Fixed Moves | Concepts over techniques — adapt to what the opponent gives you |

## Curriculum Overview

**Three Empty Hand Forms:**
1. **Siu Nim Tao** (Little Idea) — Foundation: stance, structure, basic hand positions, Tan/Bong/Fook Sau
2. **Chum Kiu** (Seeking the Bridge) — Footwork, closing distance, waist power
3. **Biu Jee** (Thrusting Fingers) — Advanced emergency form, circle stepping, elbow strikes

**Chi Sao (Sticky Hands):** The heart of Ving Tsun training. Partners maintain constant arm contact and develop tactile sensitivity, reflexes, and adaptive responses. Not sparring — a sensitivity laboratory that bridges theory and real application.

**Wooden Dummy (Muk Yan Jong):** Trains distancing, angles, footwork, and flowing transitions between techniques.

**Weapons:**
- Butterfly Knives (Double Knives) — close-range, double-weapon principles
- Long Pole (Dragon Staff) — long-range, single-weapon principles

## Sifu Wong Kin Wai — Polished Bio

Sifu Wong Kin Wai is a certified Ving Tsun instructor and the first representative of the Shun Mo Scientific Ving Tsun lineage in the United States. He holds certification from the Ving Tsun Athletic Association and is a direct student of Sifu Chan Kim Man — one of the most respected masters of the Wong Shun Leung system, who trained under Grandmaster Wong Shun Leung for nearly two decades.

Through years of dedicated study, Sifu Wong developed a deep command of Ving Tsun's core principles: centerline theory, economy of motion, and tactile sensitivity through Chi Sao. His training was shaped not only by technical drilling but by a thorough understanding of the concepts that make the system effective against fully resisting opponents.

As an instructor, Sifu Wong is known for his clear, patient, and methodical approach. He emphasizes hands-on learning — guiding each student through structured drills and progressive partner work to build real skill, not just choreography. His goal is to give every student a genuine foundation: practical self-defense ability, improved physical fitness, and the confidence that comes from testing yourself in a safe, supportive environment.

Bringing an authentic line of one of Ving Tsun's most battle-tested lineages to Boston, Sifu Wong teaches the art as both a highly effective combat system and a practice of ongoing personal development.
